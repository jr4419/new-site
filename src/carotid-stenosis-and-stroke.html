Carotid stenosis is narrowing of the carotid arteries, usually from atherosclerosis. It can reduce blood flow to the brain or send clots and plaque debris to the brain. A stroke occurs when blood flow to part of the brain is interrupted, causing brain injury.

Symptoms of stroke

  • Sudden weakness or numbness, especially on one side
  • Facial droop
  • Slurred speech or trouble speaking
  • Vision loss
  • Dizziness or loss of balance

Diagnosis

  • Carotid ultrasound
  • CT angiography or MR angiography
  • Brain CT or MRI if a stroke is suspected

Treatment

  • Antiplatelet therapy
  • Cholesterol-lowering medications
  • Control of blood pressure, diabetes, and smoking
  • Surgical intervention — carotid endarterectomy or carotid stenting for significant or symptomatic stenosis

Stroke can be devastating, but many cases are preventable. A vascular surgeon can perform carotid ultrasound screening based on your risk factors to detect stenosis early. If narrowing is found, a monitoring and preventive plan can be put in place, and newer carotid interventions can be tailored to your situation if surgery is needed.
