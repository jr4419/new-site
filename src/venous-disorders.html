Venous disorders arise when the veins and their one-way valves fail to return blood efficiently to the heart, allowing blood to pool in the legs and pelvis. They range from cosmetic spider veins to painful varicose veins, chronic venous insufficiency, leg ulcers, and the potentially dangerous clots of deep vein thrombosis. Most are highly treatable with minimally invasive, in-office procedures. Select a condition below to learn more.
