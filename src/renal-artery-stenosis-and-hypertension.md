## Symptoms and clues

- Difficult-to-control hypertension
- Sudden worsening of blood pressure
- Abdominal or flank bruit
- Rise in creatinine after starting an ACE inhibitor or ARB
- Recurrent flash pulmonary edema in severe cases

## Diagnosis

- Duplex renal artery ultrasound
- CT angiography or MR angiography
- Renal angiography, sometimes, if intervention is being considered

## Treatment

- Blood pressure control with medications
- Statins and risk-factor management if atherosclerotic disease is present
- Angioplasty with or without stenting in selected cases, especially fibromuscular dysplasia or severe, symptomatic disease
