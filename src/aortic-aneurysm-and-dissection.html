An aortic aneurysm is an abnormal dilation or bulging of the aorta due to weakness of the vessel wall. An aortic dissection is a tear in the inner layer of the aortic wall that allows blood to split the layers of the wall apart.

Why they're serious

Both can lead to rupture, severe bleeding, shock, and death.

Common risk factors: hypertension, atherosclerosis, smoking, connective tissue disorders, trauma, and older age.

Typical symptoms

  • Aneurysm: often silent until it becomes large or ruptures.
  • Dissection: sudden, severe tearing chest or back pain, often with differences in blood pressure between the arms.

Diagnosis

Aneurysms are often found incidentally or during imaging done for another reason.

  • Ultrasound
  • CT angiography
  • MRI

Treatment

Aortic aneurysm

  • Small, asymptomatic aneurysm: blood pressure control, statins if indicated, and regular imaging follow-up.
  • Large or symptomatic aneurysm: surgical repair or endovascular repair — EVAR for an abdominal aneurysm when appropriate, or open surgery for some cases, especially large or complex aneurysms.

Aortic dissection

  • Type A dissection: emergency surgery.
  • Type B dissection: usually medical management first, unless complications occur — surgery or endovascular repair if there is rupture, organ ischemia, or worsening symptoms.

A vascular surgeon can help decide whether you need surgery and which type, and can monitor and help prevent life-threatening complications.
