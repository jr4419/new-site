Arterial disorders develop when the arteries that carry oxygen-rich blood become narrowed, blocked, weakened, or torn — most often from atherosclerosis. Left untreated, they can lead to heart attack, stroke, limb loss, or life-threatening bleeding. Many are silent until an advanced stage, which is why screening and early evaluation matter.
