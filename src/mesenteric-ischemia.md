## Types

- **Acute mesenteric ischemia:** a sudden loss of blood flow — a medical emergency.
- **Chronic mesenteric ischemia:** gradual narrowing of the mesenteric arteries, often causing "intestinal angina" after meals.

## Common causes

- Embolus or thrombosis in the mesenteric arteries
- Low blood pressure or low-flow states
- Atherosclerosis

## Symptoms

- Severe abdominal pain, often out of proportion to exam findings
- Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
- Blood in the stool in severe cases
- Weight loss and post-meal pain in chronic disease

## Diagnosis

- CT angiography is the key test
- Blood tests may show elevated lactate, but they are not specific

## Treatment

- **Acute:** emergency treatment with IV fluids, antibiotics, anticoagulation when appropriate, and urgent revascularization or surgery.
- **Chronic:** risk-factor control and revascularization if symptomatic.
