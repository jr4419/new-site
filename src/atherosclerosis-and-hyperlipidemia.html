Atherosclerosis and hyperlipidemia are closely related conditions that drive much of arterial disease.

  • Hyperlipidemia means elevated levels of fats in the blood — especially LDL cholesterol and/or triglycerides.
  • Atherosclerosis is the buildup of fatty plaques in the artery walls, which can narrow arteries and reduce blood flow.

High LDL cholesterol can enter the artery walls, become oxidized, and trigger inflammation. Over time this leads to plaque formation — the basis of atherosclerosis.

Why it matters

Atherosclerosis can cause coronary artery disease, heart attack, stroke, and peripheral artery disease.

Common risk factors

  • High LDL cholesterol
  • Low HDL cholesterol
  • High triglycerides
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Hypertension
  • Obesity
  • Sedentary lifestyle
  • Family history

Treatment and prevention

  • Healthy diet
  • Regular exercise
  • Weight control
  • Stopping smoking
  • Controlling blood pressure and diabetes
  • Medications: statins, ezetimibe, PCSK9 inhibitors, fibrates, bile acid sequestrants, niacin, and icosapent ethyl

A physician can help establish a long-term treatment plan to slow progression, and can recommend newer medications with fewer side effects, better results, and easier compliance.
