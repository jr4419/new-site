Varithena is a non-thermal, non-tumescent treatment for varicose veins that uses a specially formulated foam to close diseased veins from the inside. It is the only FDA-approved foam for this purpose.

How it works

Rather than using heat, Varithena relies on a two-part chemical and physical action:

  • A sclerosing agent: the foam contains polidocanol, a medication that irritates and destroys the inner lining of the vein.
  • Blood displacement: the cohesive microfoam stays together and pushes blood out of the vein as it travels, allowing the polidocanol to contact the vein wall directly for optimal effect.

Step-by-step procedure

The procedure is performed in the office and typically takes about 10 minutes to administer.

  • Ultrasound mapping: a duplex ultrasound maps the diseased veins and plans the injection sites.
  • Injection: using ultrasound guidance, the doctor injects the microfoam directly into the targeted veins (multiple sites may be used).
  • Vein destruction: the foam fills the vein, displaces blood, and delivers polidocanol to the wall, causing it to spasm and collapse. Over the following months, the body absorbs the closed vein, turning it into a thin strand of scar tissue.
  • Post-procedure: you wear a compression stocking to support healing and are encouraged to walk soon afterward.

What the clinical evidence shows

  • High success rates: anatomical closure rates are comparable to thermal ablation, with 93.5% of patients eliminating reflux. Varithena is not significantly different from laser or radiofrequency ablation for venous closure from 3 months up to 6 years.
  • Symptom relief: 94.4% of patients reported relief from leg heaviness, achiness, and swelling.
  • Complex veins: because it is a liquid foam that flows through the veins, Varithena can effectively treat twisty (tortuous) or smaller veins that are hard to reach with a laser or radiofrequency catheter.
  • Safety profile: the risk of deep vein thrombosis is equivalent to thermal ablation. The foam uses a gas mixture with extremely low nitrogen content (below 0.8%), making residual bubbles highly absorbable and reducing the risk of complications.

Varithena is an excellent option for tortuous veins and for patients who prefer to avoid heat and needles for anesthesia. Dr. Qin can advise whether it's right for you.
